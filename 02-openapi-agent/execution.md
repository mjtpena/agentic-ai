## 4) Practical tips for an Australian visiting Japan 🇦🇺➡️🇯🇵

### Transport
- Tokyo’s rail network is excellent but can be overwhelming at first.
- Get a **Suica/PASMO**-type IC card for easy tap-and-go travel.
- Google Maps works very well for trains and walking routes.
- Peak-hour trains are extremely busy—if possible, avoid 7:30–9:00 AM and 5:30–7:30 PM.

### Cultural etiquette
- Keep your voice low on trains.
- Don’t eat while walking in crowded areas unless it’s clearly normal for the setting.
- Queue neatly and wait your turn.
- Tipping is generally **not expected** in Japan.
- Be mindful with rubbish—bins are scarce, so carry a small bag for waste.

### If you’re staying in Tokyo next week
Because the weather is cool, damp, and occasionally windy:
- Plan indoor backups: museums, shopping districts, cafés, depachika food halls
- Build extra time into your day for rain delays and station navigation
- Use weatherproof footwear to stay comfortable on long walking days

### Safety and comfort
- Tokyo is very safe, but keep an eye on belongings in crowded stations.
- Carry a portable charger; navigation and transit apps will drain battery quickly.
- If you’re jet-lagged from Australia, schedule a lighter first day and get daylight exposure early.
